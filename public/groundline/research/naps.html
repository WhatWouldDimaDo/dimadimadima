5 · Permit-trail constraint (vertical structural note)

  • Prior-work finding (Jul 16 pools sizing): in Fulton/DeKalb, pool permits route through county Board of Health / Environmental Health, not the standard building portals — permit-scrapers (Shovels et al.) miss them; renovations often pull no permit at all.
  • Today's nuance [verified — Fulton County BoH]: Fulton BoH Environmental Health permits and inspects public pools (1,800+ of them, annual permits, plan review before construction). Private residential pools are explicitly outside the BoH routine permitting program; residential pool construction permits go through municipal building departments (City of Atlanta, Sandy Springs, etc.), and renovation work frequently pulls no permit.
  • Net implication (unchanged): permit-trail data is a weak discovery/lead-gen channel in this vertical, for us and for competitors. Visibility in this market is referral + review + AI-answer presence — which strengthens the case that NAPSI's invisibility is the highest-leverage problem, and that this audit can't be replicated by a permit-scraping shop.
