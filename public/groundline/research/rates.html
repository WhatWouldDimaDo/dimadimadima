What drives fee levels across all six models

Across every category, four factors move the price:

  1. Exclusivity — a lead shared eight ways costs less than one delivered to a single contractor.
  2. Scope of work behind the lead — a phone number costs less than a qualified prospect; a qualified prospect costs less than one delivered with creative, follow-up, and a closing asset.
  3. Who carries the risk — flat retainers put campaign risk on the client; performance models put it on the provider, and price higher per result for exactly that reason.
  4. Attribution — models that can prove which revenue they generated (tracked links, per-address assets) command results-based pricing; models that can't default to flat fees.

No single benchmark category covers this combination; the models above each price one slice of it. The structures most commonly used when a provider spans several slices are hybrids: a monthly base (retainer-style) combined with a results fee on attributed business (performance-style) — with attribution rules, exclusions for existing channels, and time windows defined up front.
