Part 1 — The 5-minute check, simulated

A 60-something owner of a referral-only, $100–400K pool company Googles "Dima Perkis" the night before a meeting. Here is what happens.

Result 1: LinkedIn (linkedin.com/in/dima-perkis)

What he sees:

  • Headline: VP, Data & AI | AI Strategy at the Board Level, Deployed Systems at the Team Level | Mailchimp, Equifax, Accenture | Duke MBA
  • About: enterprise arc — AI startup, LLM judges, Equifax AI Maturity Assessment, $120M at Mailchimp, $200M Accenture chatbot program. Closer: agents running his inbox, map stickers for his 4-year-old.
  • Experience: Lahzo (Mar–Jun 2026), Equifax, Mailchimp, Accenture, A&M, Booz Allen.

What lands:

  • Mailchimp. The one brand a home-services owner actually knows — and knows as the small-business marketing company. This is the strongest existing bridge on the profile and it's already in the headline.
  • "Deployed Systems at the Team Level." Owners are allergic to consultants who talk; this phrase says he builds. It works for both audiences — leave it alone.
  • The closer paragraph. The 4-year-old's map stickers is the only human moment on the page. A family-business owner reads that line and likes the guy.

What confuses:

  • "AI Strategy at the Board Level," "Duke MBA," "Equifax," "LLM judges" — that's the vocabulary of someone who costs $400/hr and wants a W-2, not someone who gets pool builders more $300K jobs.
  • Nothing — zero words — connects to trades, home services, contractors, or local business. The Lahzo entry is about an AI sales startup and conversation intelligence.
  • The dates (Lahzo ended Jun 2026, nothing after) read as "between jobs." That plants the exact doubt the dating test can't afford: "Is this a real service, or a corporate guy filling time until his next VP gig?"

Result 2: dimadimadima.com

What he sees: masthead homepage — "I lead teams that turn emerging capabilities into revenue-producing systems," $140M+ / 15+ proof points, then a wall of projects: AI Ideas galaxy, ATL Radar, Spotify analysis, ToddlerMaps, Suno songs.

What lands: prolific, real, hands-on. Nobody who fakes AI competence has this site. It reads "this guy builds things for fun," which is disarming and credible.

What confuses: it's a portfolio for the curious, not for a buyer. There is no home-services anything visible. Groundline is live on the site but unlinked and robots-disallowed — invisible to this search on purpose, which means the one page built for him is the one page he can't find.

Result 3: nothing else

No Groundline in search results (robots-disallowed, unlinked). No posts about trades. The live proposal demo (landscapingdemo-nine.vercel.app) is unfindable without a direct link.

Verdict

Fails the dating test today — on relevance, not credibility. Credibility is over-supplied: two properties full of proof that he's a serious AI operator. Relevance is zero: not one public sentence says "I do this for businesses like yours." The gap closes with four additive edits and one consistency fix. No rewrite required.

Part 2 — Surgical edits

Ordered by leverage. Each one: exact copy, where it goes, risk to the enterprise lane.

Edit 1 — About section: one bridging closer line

Where: LinkedIn About, appended as the final line of the existing closer paragraph (after "…and to the businesses I work with.").

Exact copy:

Right now that includes an independent practice deploying AI growth systems for high-end home-services companies in Atlanta — precision homeowner targeting, photorealistic property-specific design proposals, and attribution tracked through to the signed job.

Why this placement: the existing closer already pivots from enterprise to personal building ("…bringing those to my own life and to the businesses I work with"). This line completes that sentence's promise with a concrete example instead of opening a new topic. The trades owner who skims to the bottom finds his industry named. The enterprise reader finds a candidate who stayed in motion after Lahzo.

Risk to enterprise lane: LOW — arguably positive. Recruiters read active consulting during a transition as momentum, not distraction. "Independent practice" (not "startup," not "company I founded") signals it doesn't compete with a full-time offer. Do not use the word "founder" here.

Edit 2 — New Experience entry for the independent practice

Where: LinkedIn Experience, new entry. It will sit above Lahzo chronologically — that's unavoidable and fine.

Title problem: "Groundline" is a working name; don't commit it to a LinkedIn company field (that creates a company-page stub and makes a rename a public event). Use LinkedIn's "Self-employed" employment type with a descriptive title.

Exact copy:

AI Growth Systems for High-End Home Services · Independent Practice / Self-employed · Jul 2026 – Present · Atlanta, Georgia

Independent practice deploying AI growth systems for high-end residential trades — landscaping, pools, outdoor construction.

Currently running a live pilot with a high-end Atlanta landscaping company: AI-targeted homeowner identification from property records and neighborhood signals, photorealistic per-address design proposals delivered as personal microsites, and engagement tracking from first visit through to the signed job. Skills: AI Systems · Growth Strategy · Marketing Attribution · Local Business Marketing

Three sentences, results-shaped, no methodology lecture, no dollar figures. When the practice gets its real name, the title field is a 10-second edit.

Risk to enterprise lane: MEDIUM — the riskiest edit in this audit. A Jul 2026 self-employed entry at the top of Experience is the first thing a recruiter sees, and a lazy read is "he started a business — is he actually available?" Mitigations, all load-bearing:

  • "Independent Practice," never "Founder & CEO." Practice = portable; company = committed.
  • Keep it three sentences. A long entry reads as the main event; a tight one reads as what serious operators do between roles.
  • The About section (Edit 1) frames it first, on Dima's terms, before the recruiter hits the Experience list.
  • Accepted trade-off: this entry is also the single highest-value item for the dating test — it's the difference between "guy with a hobby" and "guy with a practice and a live pilot." The enterprise cost is small and the trades gain is the whole game. Do it.

"Why Meet With Me" one-sheet — template (own section)

Pattern: the DuPont play — walk in with one sheet of paper that proves you already did the homework on their business. No deck. Results, never methodology. Close with a fixed-menu choice, never an open-ended "let's explore."

Usage notes (not printed):

  • Fill the SPOTLIGHT section per target from the geo-audit before every meeting. Everything else is stable.
  • Zero dollar figures anywhere. If a number isn't a real observed result, it doesn't go on the sheet.
  • One page printed. If it spills, cut from the proof blocks — never from the spotlight.
  • Flavor 1 is a paid working session ("first engagement: a paid ~2-hour working session"). This intentionally supersedes the free 1-hour assessment on the Groundline site for warm-intro targets — free is for cold traffic, paid is for referred owners who need to take it seriously.
  • Bracketed [...] = fill per target. Everything unbracketed is verified and reusable.
